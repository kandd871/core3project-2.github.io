In the land of whimsy,
				where nonsense roams,
	A pointless poem finds its playful home.
	Words waltz without purpose,
		rhyme without reason,
In a lyrical dance,
		it's the silliest season.
	A cat wears a hat made of bubblegum pink,
And the stars in the sky enjoy a sweet drink.
			The sun wears sunglasses,
the moon does a jig,
													Oh,
					what a world, 
						where the absurd is big.

	Bananas recite Shakespeare,
		apples wear shoes,
Raindrops converse with the singing blues.
			Mountains wear scarves,
			and oceans wear hats,
				In this nonsensical world,
										imagine that!

		No meaning,
no sense,
		just words in the air,
	A pointless poem,
						beyond compare.
So let it be known,
					in this verse so wild,
	Sometimes the best poems are entirely mild.
